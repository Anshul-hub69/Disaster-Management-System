Standard Response Protocol

Operations Timeline

The eight-stage protocol every disaster zone moves through, from first alert to full recovery.

✓

1. Alert

Disaster signal received from IMD, NDMA or ground reporting network and logged into the grid.

Kaziranga Belt · Puri Coastal Strip · Bhuj Fault Line
✓

2. Assessment

Rapid severity scoring, affected population estimate and required resource profile generated.

Severity confirmed within 45 minutes on average
✓

3. Volunteer Assignment

Matched volunteers notified by skill, proximity and availability; assignment confirmed on the dashboard.

27–31 volunteers deployed per critical zone
●

4. Resource Dispatch

Relief kits, medical supplies and rescue equipment routed from the nearest depot to the zone.

In progress — Puri Coastal Strip, Bandipur Range
5

5. Rescue

On-ground extraction and evacuation of affected people to designated safe zones.

6

6. Medical Support

Triage, first aid and hospital referral for injuries sustained during the disaster event.

7

7. Recovery

Rehabilitation support, shelter transition and resource wind-down as the zone stabilises.

8

8. Completed

Zone closed on the grid; volunteers released back to the available pool for the next deployment.
